# Concept Paper: Afromeet

## Vision

Afromeet provides a simple, dependable way for distributed African teams and communities to connect through video meetings with minimal setup time and strong performance on constrained networks.

## Objectives

- Reduce the time to create and join a meeting.
- Provide a trustworthy pre-join experience for device checks.
- Deliver a clear, minimal meeting room experience with core collaboration tools.

## Target Users

- Remote teams and startups coordinating across regions.
- Community groups organizing virtual events or classes.
- Educators and facilitators hosting live sessions.

## Value Proposition

- Lightweight browser-based experience with no install required.
- Reliable audio/video with realtime chat for continuity.
- Simple meeting links that are easy to share.

## Key Capabilities

- Meeting scheduling and shareable links.
- Pre-join lobby with mic/camera controls.
- Live meeting room with participant list and chat.
- Responsive UI that works across desktop and mobile.

## Scope (In)

- Browser-based joining and hosting.
- Basic meeting moderation controls (mute/self controls).
- Simple participant presence and activity feedback.

## Scope (Out)

- Advanced moderation roles and permissions.
- Recording, transcription, or AI summaries.
- Large webinar-style broadcasting features.

## Assumptions

- Users have access to modern browsers with WebRTC support.
- Real-time signaling is provided via a managed service.
- Media delivery is peer-based in the initial phase.

## Success Criteria

- Median join time under 10 seconds on typical connections.
- Stable audio for at least 95% of sessions.
- Positive qualitative feedback about simplicity and speed.
